How Do Cash Home Buyers Handle Pre-1978 Homes?

As DC homeowners know, pre-1978 homes require federal lead-paint disclosure and may have Seller's Property Condition Disclosure requirements.

Parlevu Global Services LLC understands these regulations and will guide you through the process to ensure a smooth sale.

What Trinidad Homeowners Should Know First

DC requires a Seller's Property Condition Disclosure for most residential sales, and pre-1978 homes carry federal lead-paint disclosure duties. That matters no matter how you sell — to a cash buyer, through an agent, or on your own. Being upfront about the property's condition protects you legally and actually speeds up a cash sale, because investors price the repairs in from day one.

DC foreclosures are judicial and slower than in Maryland or Virginia, but interest, fees, and stress compound the longer you wait. Timing is the single biggest lever you control. The earlier you explore your options, the more of them you have.

How a Cash Sale Actually Works

The process is simpler than most sellers expect: you share the property address and a few details, receive a written offer (usually within 24-48 hours), pick your own closing date, and sell completely as-is — no repairs, no cleaning, no showings, no agent commissions.

DC transfer and recordation taxes are among the region's highest (up to 2.9% combined), which makes avoiding agent commissions especially valuable. A reputable buyer will walk you through the numbers line by line so you know exactly what you'll net at closing before you sign anything.
