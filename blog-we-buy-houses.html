What Does "We Buy Houses" Really Mean? A Baltimore Homeowner's Guide

You've seen the signs on telephone poles, the postcards in your mailbox, the ads on social media: "We Buy Houses — Any Condition — Cash in 7 Days." But what does it actually mean? Is it legitimate? And is it the right move for your situation? This guide cuts through the noise and gives Baltimore homeowners the straight truth about cash home buyers.

How Cash Home Buying Actually Works

Cash home buyers — also called real estate investors or direct buyers — purchase properties directly from homeowners without using traditional bank financing. They pay with their own capital (or private lending), which allows them to move fast and skip the requirements that slow down conventional sales.

Here's what happens from start to finish:

Step 1

You Submit Your Property Information

You provide basic info: address, condition, your timeline, and any special circumstances (foreclosure, probate, divorce, vacancy, etc.). No cleaning, no staging, no Sunday open houses required.

Step 2

You Receive a No-Obligation Cash Offer

The buyer evaluates the property's after-repair value (ARV), estimated repair costs, local comparable sales, and their overhead to generate a fair cash offer — usually within 24–48 hours. No appraisal contingencies, no financing approval delays.

Step 3

You Choose Your Closing Date and Get Paid

You pick the closing date that works for your schedule — as fast as 7–14 days or as late as 60 days if you need time. On closing day, you receive your full cash payment. No last-minute lender conditions, no buyer backing out.

Who Benefits Most From a Cash Sale?

🏚️ Distressed Properties

Homes needing major repairs that would fail a traditional buyer's inspection or lender appraisal.

⚡ Urgent Timelines

Job relocation, divorce, foreclosure threat, or medical situations requiring a fast, certain close.

🏠 Inherited Properties

Estate properties where heirs want a clean, fast exit without managing repairs or showings.

📦 Landlords Exiting

Tired landlords with problem tenants or deferred maintenance who want out without the headache.

True Cost Comparison: Cash Offer vs. Traditional Listing

At first glance, a cash offer looks lower. But when you account for all the costs of a traditional sale, the gap often narrows significantly:

Cost Factor Traditional Listing Cash Sale
Agent Commissions 5–6% of sale price $0
Repairs & Updates $5,000–$30,000+ $0 — sold as-is
Closing Costs (seller) 1–3% Often $0
Carrying Costs (mortgage, taxes, utilities while listed) $3,000–$8,000 avg. Near $0
Price Reductions / Buyer Concessions Common None
Certainty of Close ~75–80% ~99%

How to Spot Scams vs. Legitimate Cash Buyers

Not all "We Buy Houses" companies are created equal. Here are the warning signs of a predatory operation — and what a reputable buyer looks like:

🚩 Red Flags

  • ✗Requires upfront fees before any offer
  • ✗Pressures you to sign same day
  • ✗No verifiable business address or reviews
  • ✗Extremely lowball offer with no explanation
  • ✗Asks you to sign over the deed early

✅ Legitimate Signs

  • ✓Registered LLC with verifiable address
  • ✓No upfront fees — ever
  • ✓Explains how they calculated your offer
  • ✓Gives you time to review, no pressure
  • ✓Uses a licensed title company at closing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is "We Buy Houses" legit or a scam?

Legitimate cash home buying companies are real businesses with verifiable registrations, physical addresses, and transparent processes. Scams typically require upfront fees, pressure quick signatures, or are impossible to verify. Parlevu Global Services LLC is a registered Maryland LLC at 3333 Windsor Ave, Baltimore — you can verify us with the Maryland State Department of Assessments and Taxation.

How fast can I sell my Baltimore house for cash?

Most cash buyers in Baltimore can close in 7–30 days depending on the company and your preferences. Parlevu Global Services typically closes within 14–21 days. If you need more time, we can also accommodate longer timelines — you choose your date.

Do cash buyers pay fair prices for Baltimore homes?

Cash buyers typically offer 70–85% of a home's after-repair market value. While that's below retail listing price, sellers save on agent commissions (5–6%), repairs, carrying costs during a 60–90 day listing, closing costs, and the risk of a deal falling through. For many sellers, especially those with distressed properties, the net difference is smaller than it appears.

Do I need to make repairs before selling for cash?

No. Cash buyers purchase homes in any condition — including homes with foundation issues, roof damage, fire damage, hoarding situations, or code violations. You sell exactly as-is. No cleaning, no repairs, no staging required.

What happens at closing when I sell to a cash buyer?

Closing works through a licensed Maryland title company. The title company verifies ownership, ensures there are no liens or title issues that would affect the transfer, prepares the closing documents, and disburses your funds. You'll receive a certified check or wire transfer on the day of closing.
