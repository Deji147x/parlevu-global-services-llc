What About Foreclosures?

What Washington DC Homeowners Should Know First

DC requires a Seller's Property Condition Disclosure for most residential sales, and pre-1978 homes carry federal lead-paint disclosure duties. That matters no matter how you sell — to a cash buyer, through an agent, or on your own. Being upfront about the property's condition protects you legally and actually speeds up a cash sale, because investors price the repairs in from day one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to disclose liens or code violations on my property?

Yes, in Washington DC, sellers are required to provide a Seller's Property Condition Disclosure statement with most residential sales. This includes any outstanding liens or code violations on the property.

Can I sell my house with outstanding liens without making repairs?

Not necessarily. While you may not be required to make repairs, you can still factor the lien into the sale price. However, this may affect the overall value of your home and reduce its appeal to potential buyers.

What's the best way to sell my house with code violations?

The best approach depends on the severity of the code violation and the seller's willingness to make repairs. Working with a reputable cash home buyer like us can help you navigate this process and get a fair price for your property.
