## What their top-performing content targets (topic → keyword pattern)

1. **Situation long-tails (highest intent, lowest competition)** — foreclosure/pre-foreclosure, inherited/probate/trust sales, divorce, liens & code violations, fire/water/mold/foundation damage, hoarder houses, bad tenants/vacant homes, underwater mortgage, bankruptcy, reverse mortgage, HOA problems, relocation, retirees/downsizing.
   - Pattern: "Selling a house with {problem} in {state}" / "Can you sell a house {situation} in {state}?"
2. **Process/education short-tails** — how cash buyers work, how offers are calculated, closing costs, paperwork, FSBO vs agent, staging costs, market timing, avoiding scams ("can you trust we-buy-houses companies").
3. **Geo pages** — "{service} in {city}" for every suburb: Express and DBC tag every post with Baltimore/NoVA/DC/Upper Marlboro/Bowie.
4. **Comparison/trust content** — cash sale vs listing, legitimacy checks, "top companies" (ride the listicle demand).

## Structure of winning posts
- Title = exact keyword + benefit/number ("Who Pays Closing Costs When Selling A House In Virginia")
- 800–1500 words, H2s phrased as questions (FAQ-schema friendly)
- State-specific legal detail (disclosure forms, probate rules) — this is what beats national listicles
- Internal links up to a state/city hub page and across sibling posts
- CTA: address-capture form + phone number, repeated mid-post and at end; "no obligation / no repairs / no fees" language

## Strategy for Parlevu (what the 120-post plan exploits)
1. Own the **situation × state matrix** (13 situations × MD/VA/DC) that only Express covers well.
2. Own the **city long-tail** your Maps rivals ignore (Baltimore neighborhoods + MD/VA/DC suburbs).
3. Question-formatted H2s + FAQPage schema on every post (already your house style).
4. Every post links up to a state hub page → hub links to contact — clean silo for local relevance.
5. GBP reviews are your rivals' main asset — content + reviews together beat either alone.
